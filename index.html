About smoking

Smoking is the inhalation and exhalation of smoke from burning tobacco in cigars, cigarettes and pipes which has a history starting in the late 1500's. Originally, smoking was as a practice followed by natives of the Western Hemisphere for religious rituals and medicinal purposes.

Today, smoking is very common. In 2011, an estimated 19% of U.S. adults were cigarette smokers. Additionally, about 18% of high school students smoked cigarettes.

Over 4,000 chemicals such as nicotine, tar, and carbon monoxide, ammonia, hydrogen cyanide, and many others are present in cigarettes. This also includes 43 known cancer-causing compounds and 400 other toxins. Furthermore, Cigarettes contain nicotine, which is a highly addictive drug.
